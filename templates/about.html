// MESONET ARCHITECTURE
LAYER 01
Conv2D + BatchNorm + MaxPool
8 filters of 3×3 with ReLU activation, followed by batch normalization to stabilize gradients. 2×2 max pooling halves spatial dimensions to 128×128.
Conv2D(8, 3×3) → BN → MaxPool(2×2)
LAYER 02
Conv2D + BatchNorm + MaxPool
8 filters of 5×5 capture mid-scale artifacts. Larger kernels detect blending boundaries and GAN-generated texture inconsistencies at 64×64 resolution.
Conv2D(8, 5×5) → BN → MaxPool(2×2)
LAYER 03
Conv2D + BatchNorm + MaxPool
16 filters of 5×5 at 32×32 resolution. The network learns high-level features like lighting inconsistencies, unnatural skin texture, and edge artifacts common in deepfakes.
Conv2D(16, 5×5) → BN → MaxPool(2×2)
LAYER 04
Conv2D + BatchNorm + MaxPool (4×4)
16 filters with aggressive 4×4 pooling compress spatial info into an 8×8 feature map. Forces the model to capture only the most discriminative artifact signatures.
Conv2D(16, 5×5) → BN → MaxPool(4×4)
LAYER 05
Flatten + Dense(16) + Dense(1)
Feature maps are flattened and passed through a 16-neuron dense layer before the final sigmoid output neuron. Output ≈ 1 means Real, ≈ 0 means Fake (MesoNet convention).
Flatten → Dense(16) → Dense(1, sigmoid)
SCORING
Score Interpretation
Raw sigmoid output is inverted: AI% = (1 - raw_score) × 100. A raw score near 1 = Real, near 0 = Fake. This maps naturally to an "AI influence percentage" for display.
ai_pct = (1 - raw_score) × 100

// FAQ
What types of deepfakes does MesoNet detect? ▾
MesoNet was specifically trained on FaceForensics++ datasets covering face-swap deepfakes (DeepFaceLab, FaceSwap) and face reenactment. It works best on facial deepfakes. Fully AI-generated portraits (Stable Diffusion, DALL·E, Midjourney) may not be detected as reliably since they weren't in the original training set.
Why does it sometimes get it wrong? ▾
No detection model is perfect. MesoNet was trained on a specific dataset and may struggle with heavily compressed images, images where no face is detected (falls back to full image), very high-quality deepfakes, or real photos taken in unusual lighting. The confidence metric reflects this uncertainty.
How is video detection different from image detection? ▾
For videos, every 10th frame is sampled and analyzed individually. The final AI influence score is the mean score across all sampled frames. This provides robustness against momentary artifacts while remaining computationally practical.
Are uploaded files stored or retained? ▾
No. Files are saved temporarily to process the prediction and deleted immediately after — even if an error occurs, the finally block in app.py ensures cleanup. No images or videos are retained on the server.
Where can I read the original MesoNet paper? ▾
The original paper is "MesoNet: a Compact Facial Video Forgery Detection Network" by Darius Afchar et al. (2018). It is available on arXiv at arxiv.org/abs/1809.00888. The paper provides full details on the architecture, training data, and benchmark results.
