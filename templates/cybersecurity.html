// DEFENSE PROTOCOLS

How to Protect Yourself

Actionable steps individuals and organizations can take to defend against AI-powered threats

01

Verify Before You Trust

Always confirm unexpected video calls or voice requests through a secondary channel (call back on a known number). Never authorize transfers or share credentials based solely on an AV call.

02

Use Detection Tools

Deploy deepfake detection software like CheckAI on incoming media — especially for verification footage, executive communications, and shared news content before acting on it.

03

Set Code Words

Establish shared secret words or phrases with executives and family members that can only be known by real humans — a low-tech but highly effective anti-deepfake safeguard.

04

Check Metadata & Source

Examine image EXIF data and reverse image search suspicious profile photos. GAN-generated faces have no history, no location data, and often appear on multiple fake accounts.

05

Limit Your Digital Footprint

Reduce publicly available audio and video of yourself. Keep high-quality voice recordings private — the less material attackers can train on, the harder it is to clone you convincingly.

06

Train Your Team

Run regular security awareness sessions that include deepfake examples. Employees should know the telltale visual and audio artifacts — blurry edges, unnatural blinking, audio sync issues.
